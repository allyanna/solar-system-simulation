Neptune

Structure

This planet is an ice giant in the outer solar system. Most of the planet's mass is made up of a hot dense fluid of water, methane, and ammonia, and a small, rocky core. Scientists think there might be an ocean of super hot water under the planet’s cold clouds that is contained by the incredibly high pressure of this planet.

Surface

This planet doesn’t have a solid surface. Its atmosphere extends to great depths, gradually merging into water and other melted ices over a heavier, solid core.

Atmosphere

The atmosphere of this planet is made up mostly of hydrogen and helium with just a little bit of methane. Winds can be nine times stronger than Earth's as they whip clouds of frozen methane across the planet.
