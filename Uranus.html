Uranus

Structure

This planet is one of two ice giants in the outer solar system). Most of the planet's mass is made up of a hot dense fluid of "icy" materials—water, methane and ammonia—above a small rocky core.

Surface

As an ice giant, this planet doesn’t have a true surface. The planet is mostly swirling fluids.

Atmosphere

The atmosphere of this planet is mostly hydrogen and helium, with a small amount of methane and traces of water and ammonia.
Wind speeds can reach up to 560 miles per hour and are retrograde at the equator, blowing in the reverse direction of the planet’s rotation. Closer to the poles, winds shift to a prograde direction, flowing with the planet’s rotation.
